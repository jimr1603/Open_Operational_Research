A short one today as I just wanted to see these two graphs.

The Oatmeal has a free game with his usual humour. It’s built on making words from the given letters, and one level only lets you play two letter words.

I found an English dictionary-file at github, so let’s load it and keep the two letter words.

two_letter <- read_lines("https://raw.githubusercontent.com/dwyl/english-words/master/words_alpha.txt") 
two_letter <- tibble(word = two_letter) %>% 
  filter(str_length(word) == 2)

There are \(26^2=674\) possible 2-letter words, but only 427 of them are realised. “ta” is a word, “tf” is not.

A little tidymagic will visualise this:

two_letter %>% 
  mutate(letter_1 = str_extract(word, "^.")) %>% 
  mutate(letter_2 = str_extract(word, ".$")) %>% 
  ggplot(aes(x=letter_1, y=letter_2)) + geom_tile() + coord_fixed() +
    labs(title = "Two-letter English Words")

Or some plotly magic:

two_letter %>% 
  mutate(letter_1 = str_extract(word, "^.")) %>% 
  mutate(letter_2 = str_extract(word, ".$")) %>% 
  plot_ly(x=~letter_1, y=~letter_2) %>% 
    add_markers(text=~word, hoverinfo="text")
